Proposed Methodology

To analyse the data we will use both bar charts and contour maps. Bar charts will be used to determine variances between categories (e.g. genres, film actors, ratings vs revenue). Contour maps will allow us to visualise whether there is a concentration of films at a particular film rating/ box-office revenue. Scatter graphs may also be used to determine an overall trend. What statistical analysis methods we used

Spearman's Rank and Kendall's Tau

We used Python to find the Spearman and Kendall’s Tau correlation coefficients. This assigns a numerical value to the correlation and explains how strong/weak it is. From this information we will also know whether to accept or reject our hypotheses.

Spearman’s rank is a non-parametric statistical test and is used instead of Pearson’s product-moment correlation when data is not normally distributed. It is used to determine the strength of a monotonic relationship (either when the value of one variable increases, so does the value of the other; or when the value of one variable increases, the other decreases).


Figure 1: example of monotonic and non-monotonic relationships


Coefficient values will be between -1 and +1. +1 denotes a perfect positive correlation, -1 denotes a perfect negative correlation and 0 denotes no correlation at all. It is calculated using the following equation:

In the equation d is the difference between ranks and n is the number of ranks

Kendall's tau is a non-parametric test similar to Spearman’s Rank. It is used when many score have the same rank e.g. for our film ratings.

We will be interpreting a very weak correlation as anything between 0 and 0.19; between 0.2 and 0.39 as a weak correlation; between 0.4 and 0.59 as a moderate correlation; between 0.6 and 0.79 as a strong correlation and between 0.8 and 1.0 as a very strong correlation.

Spearman's Rank vs. Kendall's Tau

Interpretations of the two tests are very similar but Spearman’s is more widely used. Kendall’s tau p-value is more accurate if there is a smaller sample size which is why we also calculated Spearman’s rank. However, there is evidence to suggest that Kendall’s tau is a better estimate of correlation than Spearman’s (Field, 2005). Kendall’s is also much less sensitive to error. Furthermore, Kendall’s tau is calculated based on comparing pairs of data points and seeing if they ‘match’, whereas spearman’s is based on deviations.

For our project we therefore chose to use both given that for some variables, many scores had the same rank but for others they did not. Using both tests will also pick up differences in accuracies between the two and strengthen overlaps in results.

2. Analysis on relationships

We will be focusing to determine the relationship between film ratings and their revenue to test our hypothesis. Meanwhile we will also look at some other relationships, including gender of characters and revenue, as well as budget and revenue.

For the analysis of revenue and ratings, we will utilise cross tabulation to determine the relationship between the two variables in depth. For the analysis of each of the 3 combinations, we will examine the relationship visually using scatterplot, bar-graphs and violin-graphs. All graphs are plotted with the horizontal axis being film rating, gender and budget respectively for each combination, and the vertical axis being film revenue in billions. This is because we consider film revenue to be the dependent variable and rating, gender, budget to be the independent variable (Cleveland, 1993). For the graphs with independent variable being categorical, including bar charts and violin chart, the film revenue has been converted to average revenue for each interval on the horizontal axis to be able to construct the graph. Since the films under the same interval on the horizontal axis are generalised into one part, the revenue they obtained have to be integrated also.

Statistical Analysis

Correlation Analysis

Correlation analysis depends to some extent on making assumptions about the collected data and choosing the most suitable way of comparing that data. The performance of the correlation analysis methods depends on the form of the analysed data and how it relates to the approach being used. There are parametric and non-parametric test methods. Parametric ones, such as Linear Regression and Pearson Correlation Coefficient, assume that sample data comes from a population that follows a probability distribution based on a fixed set of parameters. Conversely, a non-parametric test, such as Kendall’s Tau and Spearman’s Rank Correlation Coefficient, is suitable for data for which the parameter set is not fixed. Nonparametric tests work for data which is distribution-free or has a specified distribution but with the distribution's parameters unspecified.

Relying on a fixed parameter set, parametric models assume more about a given population than nonparametric methods do. When the assumptions are correct, parametric methods produce more accurate estimates compared to nonparametric methods. However, as more is assumed by a parametric test, when the assumptions are not correct they have a greater chance of failing.

In this project, the sample data collected on revenues, rankings, gender and budget is data with no specified distribution. Thus, only nonparametric tests could be used to determine the correlation between revenue and 3 different variables (rankings, gender and budget). There are two accepted measures of non-parametric rank correlations: Kendall’s tau and Spearman’s Rank Correlation Coefficient (Spearman’s rho). These two tests assess statistical associations based on the ranks of the data to measure the strength of the relationship between two variables. In most of the situations, the interpretations of Kendall’s tau and Spearman’s rho are very similar and thus lead to the same conclusions.

  • Kendall’s tau: usually smaller values than Spearman’s rho correlation, calculations based on concordant and discordant pairs, insensitive to error, P values are more accurate with smaller sample sizes, correlation coefficient between 1 and -1.
  • Spearman’s rho: usually has larger values than Kendall’s Tau, calculations based on deviations, much more sensitive to error and discrepancies in data, correlation coefficient between 1 and -1.

Kendall’s tau p-value is more accurate if there is a smaller sample size which is why we also calculated Spearman’s rho. However, there is evidence to suggest that Kendall’s tau is a better estimate of correlation than Spearman’s (Field, 2005). Kendall’s tau is also much less sensitive to error. Furthermore, Kendall’s tau is calculated based on comparing pairs of data points and seeing if they ‘match’, whereas spearman’s is based on deviations.

Since samples of revenues, rankings, gender and budget vary in size and distribution (and possibly in some other ways), both tests were conducted to pick up differences in accuracies between the two and strengthen reliability of conclusions made after verifying results.

To conduct Spearman’s rho, the following conditions must be satisfied:

  • the two variables should be measured on an ordinal, interval or ratio scale,
  • there is a monotonic relationship between the two variables.

To conduct Kendall’s tau, the following conditions must be satisfied:

  • the two variables should be measured on an ordinal or continuous scale,
  • there is a monotonic relationship between the two variables.

To conduct Spearman’s rho, the following conditions must be satisfied:

  • the two variables should be measured on an ordinal, interval or ratio scale,
  • there is a monotonic relationship between the two variables.

Data collected in this project satisfy the conditions of the two tests:

  • revenue (in dollars)
  • ratings (from TMDb)
  • gender (% male/female actors in a movie)
  • budget (in dollars)

Revenue vs Ratings

Kendall’s tau correlation coefficient (τb) = 0.209 Spearman’s rho correlation coefficient (rs) = 0.307

Kendall’s tau: there was a weak, positive correlation between revenue and ratings, which was statistically significant (τb = 0.209, p < 0.000).

Spearman’s rho: there was a weak, positive correlation between revenue and ratings, which was statistically significant (rs = 0.307, p < 0.000).

Kendall’s tau: there was a strong, positive correlation between revenue and budget, which was statistically significant (τb = 0.537, p < 0.000).

Spearman’s rho: there was a strong, positive correlation between revenue and budget, which was statistically significant (rs = 0.721, p < 0.000).
